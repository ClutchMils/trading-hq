Lesson 2: Timeframes

Why most traders get trapped

The Hard Truth

Lower timeframes feel exciting.

They move fast.

They give you more “signals”.

That’s why most beginners lose there.

Speed hides mistakes.

What a Timeframe Really Is

A timeframe is just compression of data.
  • 1D → one full trading day
  • 4H → four hours
  • 1H → one hour

Nothing changes except noise level.

Higher Timeframe

Clearer intent

Lower Timeframe

More noise

Why Higher Timeframes Matter

Big money does not trade the 1-minute chart.

They operate on:

  • Daily
  • Weekly
  • 4H
Lower timeframe moves only make sense inside higher timeframe context.

The Beginner Trap

  • Enter on 5M
  • Get stopped by noise
  • Revenge trade
  • Repeat
You’re not early. You’re on the wrong timeframe.

How Timeframes Should Be Used

Higher timeframe → direction

Lower timeframe → execution

Top-down analysis.
  • Daily → overall bias
  • 4H → key levels
  • 1H → entries
If your trade disagrees with higher timeframe, you're gambling.

Final Rule

Slow down. Zoom out. Let price show its hand.

The market rewards patience, not activity.
