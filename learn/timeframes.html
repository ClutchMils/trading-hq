The Hard Truth About Timeframes

Lower timeframes feel exciting. They move fast. They give you more "signals" That's why most beginners lose there. Speed hide mistakes.

What a Timeframe Really Is

A timeframe is just compression of data

  • 1D = one candle = one full trading day
  • 4H = one candle = four hours
  • 1H - one candle = one hour

Nothing changes except noise level. Higher timeframe = clearer intent Lower timeframe = more noise

Why Higher Timeframes Matter More

Big money does not trade the 1-minute chart.

They plan on:

  • Daily
  • Weekly
  • 4H

The lower you go, the less meaningful each moves becomes. Price on 1H matters inside the context of the Daily and 4H.

The Trap Beginners Fall into

Most Beginners:

  • Enter on 5M
  • Stop out on noise
  • Revenge trade
  • Repeat

They think they're "early." They're not early. They're wrong timeframe
