Lesson 4: Risk Management

Why this is everything

The Truth Nobody Likes

You don’t blow accounts because of bad strategy.

You blow accounts because:

  • You risk too much
  • You don’t respect drawdown
  • You let emotions size your trades

Most traders fail before their edge has a chance.

What Risk Actually Means

How much of your account you lose if you are wrong.

Risk is not confidence.

Every trade starts with a loss scenario. If you don’t know that number, you are gambling.

The Only Rule That Matters

Risk a fixed percentage per trade.

Not:

  • How good it looks
  • How bored you are
  • How close the stop is

Typical:

  • 0.5% — conservative
  • 1% — standard
  • 2% — aggressive
More than that? You're emotional, not strategic.

Small Risk vs Big Risk

Small Risk

  • Keeps you in the game
  • Protects confidence
  • Allows learning

Big Risk

  • Amplifies mistakes
  • Creates fear
  • Leads to revenge trading

Survival comes first.

Risk vs Reward

Lose small. Win bigger.

You can be wrong more than half the time and still be profitable.

Most traders lose because their losers are bigger than their winners.

Drawdown is Part of the Game

  • How deep it goes
  • Whether you can recover
If one losing streak can wipe you out, your risk is broken.

The Rule That Saves Accounts

If you feel emotional → your risk is too high.

What This Means for You

You don’t need:

  • More indicators
  • More trades
  • More confidence
You need controlled risk.
