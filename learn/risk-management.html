The Truth Nobody Likes

You don't blow accounts because of bad strategy

You blow accounts because:

  • you risk too much
  • you don't respect drawdown
  • you let emotions size your trades

Most traders fail before their edge has a chance

What Risk Actually Means

Risk is not "how confident you feel"

Risk is:

| How much of you account you lose if you are wrong.

Every trade starts with a loss scenario.

If you don't know that number, you are gambling

The Only Rule That Matters

Risk a fixed percentage per trade

Not:

  • how good it looks
  • how bored you are
  • how close the stop is

Typical:

  • 0.5% - conservative
  • 1% - standard
  • 2% - aggressive

More than that? You're emotional, not strategic

Why Small Risk Wins Long-Term

Small risk:

  • keeps you in the game
  • protects confidence
  • allows learning

Big risk:

  • amplifies mistakes
  • creates fear
  • leads to revenge trading

Survival comes first

Risk vs Reward (The Right Way)

Risk-Reward is not about being right.

it's about:

  • losing small
  • winning bigger when right

A trader can be wrong more than half the time and still be profitable.

Most beginners don't lose because they're wrong.
They lose because their losers are bigger than their winners

Drawdown is Part of the Game

Losing streaks are normal.

What matters:

  • how deep drawdown goes
  • whether you can recover

If one losing streak can wipe you out,
your risk is broken.

The Rule That Saves Accounts

If you feel emotioanl:
|Your risk is too high.

Always.

What This Means for You

You don't need:

  • more indicators
  • more trades
  • more confidence

You need controlled risk.

Everything else comes after.
